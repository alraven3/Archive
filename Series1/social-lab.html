Social Lab

The Social Labs were platforms created by the Edgelands Institute and the Mi Sangre Foundation in Medellín and Cúcuta, Colombia. The aim of the Social Labs was to create a platform for social transformation, through collaboration and conversions between different community stakeholders (young people, social sector, private sector, public sector). A series of discussions among citizens were organized around the issues of security, surveillance and the ability to reimagine the social contracts in these two cities. These discussions also dealt with questions relating to the uses and the purpose of technologies, but also on the issues of trust, compassion and ethics. These community meetings resulted in guidelines and recommendations on resource allocation, data centralization, preventive strategies, public participation, data analysis and evidence-based decision making. The aim of these recommendations is to foster collaboration in the community and to strive toward a safer, more informed and inclusive Cúcuta.
